## About WRCCDC

The Western Regional Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition (WRCCDC) is the premier collegiate cybersecurity competition serving California, Nevada, Arizona, Utah, Idaho, Oregon, Washington, Montana, Wyoming, Colorado, New Mexico, and parts of Texas. As part of the National CCDC program, WRCCDC provides students with hands-on experience defending corporate networks against professional red team attackers.

## Competition Format

### The Scenario

You and your team have just been hired as the IT and security staff for a company. You inherit an existing network infrastructure with limited documentation, unknown security posture, and potentially vulnerable systems. Your job: secure everything, keep services running, and handle business operations - all while under active attack from a professional red team.

### Key Elements

**Blue Team (Competitors)**
- 8 teams compete simultaneously
- Each team has 6-8 students
- Teams must maintain critical business services
- Handle business injects and customer requests
- Defend against red team attacks
- Document actions and create security reports

**Red Team (Professional Penetration Testers)**
- Industry professionals with extensive experience
- Use real-world attack techniques and tools
- Test defenses throughout the competition
- Provide realistic adversary pressure
- Offer educational feedback post-competition

**White Team (Competition Officials)**
- Ensure fair play and rule compliance
- Monitor scoring and infrastructure
- Adjudicate disputes
- Provide clarifications when needed

**Business Injects**
- Realistic business tasks and requests
- Customer support scenarios
- Executive requests
- Compliance requirements
- Crisis response situations

- **Outcome:** Top 8 teams advance to regionals

- **Date:** March 28-30, 2025
- **Location:** Coastline Community College, Garden Grove, CA
- **Format:** In-person, 3-day event
- **Participants:** 8 qualified teams
- **Outcome:** Winner advances to NCCDC Nationals

## Eligibility

To compete in WRCCDC, teams must:

- Be from accredited institutions in the Western Region
- Have students currently enrolled (undergrad or grad)
- Register during the registration period (see schedule)
- Comply with all NCCDC and WRCCDC rules
- Complete required paperwork and agreements

## What to Expect

### Day 1: Setup and Initial Defense
- Team check-in and orientation
- Network familiarization period (1 hour)
- Competition begins
- Initial service checks and hardening
- Red team reconnaissance phase

### Day 2: Full Operations
- Continued service maintenance
- Active red team engagement
- Business inject handling
- Incident response activities
- Team strategy adjustment

### Day 3: Final Push and Awards
- Morning competition hours
- Final scoring period
- Network shutdown procedures
- Awards ceremony
- Networking with sponsors and volunteers

## Preparation Tips

### Technical Skills to Develop

- **Operating System Hardening** - Windows and Linux security
- **Network Security** - Firewalls, IDS/IPS, network segmentation
- **Service Management** - Web servers, databases, email, DNS
- **Incident Response** - Log analysis, forensics, malware detection
- **Scripting and Automation** - Bash, PowerShell, Python
- **Security Tools** - Wireshark, nmap, Splunk, etc.

### Team Organization

- **Assign roles** - System admins, network security, incident response
- **Develop procedures** - Change control, documentation, communication
- **Practice teamwork** - Work together under pressure
- **Create runbooks** - Quick reference guides for common tasks
- **Plan communication** - How to coordinate during competition

### Resources

- Review past competition scenarios in our archive
- Participate in invitational events
- Attend WCCOMPS workshops and training sessions
- Practice with online labs and CTFs
- Study NIST Cybersecurity Framework
